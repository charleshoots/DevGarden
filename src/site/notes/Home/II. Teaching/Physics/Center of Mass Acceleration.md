> [!summary]
Center of mass in terms of acceleration describes the acceleration of the center of mass point.
> 
**Key Equations:**
> 
Center of mass acceleration:
$a_{cm}=\frac{\sum_{i}m_{i}a_{i}}{\sum_{i}m_{i}}$

>[!info]+ Read Time
**⏱ 1 min**

# Definition 
Center of mass in terms of acceleration describes the [[Acceleration\|acceleration]] of a point as if all the mass were centred on a point. By definition of [[Acceleration\|acceleration]] it's the [[Derivative\|derivative]] of the [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Center of Mass Velocity & Momentum\|center of mass velocity]].

$$
\begin{align*}
a_{cm} &= \frac{dr}{dt} v_{cm} \\
&= \frac{\sum_{i}m_{i}a_{i}}{\sum_{i}m_{i}} 
\end{align*}
$$
