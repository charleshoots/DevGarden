The centripetal force describes how an object rotates around a circle.
> 
**Key Equations:**
> 
Centripetal force:
$F_{c} = ma_{c} = \frac{mv^2}{r}$

**⏱ 1 min**

# Definition 
The centripetal force is not a new force and describes how objects rotate in a circle. The centripetal force is a center-seeking force that forces the object to rotate in a circle. This force is only valid in [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Frames of Reference\|inertial frame of references]]. Mathematically, this is described using [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Centripetal Acceleration\|centripetal acceleration]] and [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Newton Laws\|Newton's second law]]

$$
F_{c} = ma_{c} = \frac{mv^2}{r}
$$
