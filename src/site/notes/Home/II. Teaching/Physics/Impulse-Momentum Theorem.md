# Definition 
The impulse momentum theorem is a theorem that relates [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Impulse\|impulse]] and [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Linear Momentum\|linear momentum]].

## Derivation
> [!warning] Assumptions
To relate impulse and momentum, assume the following:
> - [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Impulse\|Impulse]] is defined as $I = \int_{t_{1}}^{t_{2}}F (t) dt$ 
> - The [[Instantaneous\|instantaneous change]] on [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Linear Momentum\|momentum]] can be described by [[Home/II. Teaching/Physics/Newton Laws\|newtons second law]] $F = \frac{dp}{dt}$

$$
\begin{array}{c} \\ \\
\begin{align*}
\int_{t_{1}}^{t_{2}} F(t)dt &=\int_{t_{1}}^{t_{2}} \frac{dp}{dt}dt \\
&= \int_{t_{1}}^{t_{2}} dp  \\
&= p(t_{2})-p(t_{1}) \\
&= \Delta p
\end{align*}
\\ \\

\text{Therefore:}  \\
I = \Delta p
\end{array}
$$
