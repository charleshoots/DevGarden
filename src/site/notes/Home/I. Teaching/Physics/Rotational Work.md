# Rotating Work
Rotational work measures the [[Energy\|energy]] transferred when a force moves an object rotationally. An object will only rotate when a [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Torque\|Torque]] is applied to it. So mathematically, rotational work is the integral of [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Torque\|Torque]] [[Dots Product & Angles\|dotted]] with [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Angular Displacement\|Angular Displacement]]. 

$$
W= \int_{a}^b \tau \cdot d\theta 
$$

> [!note]
There is a [[Dots Product & Angles\|dot product]] between [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Torque\|Torque]] and [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Angular Displacement\|Angular Displacement]] because only the [[Tangential & Perpendicular\|perpendicular]] force contributes to rotational motion.
