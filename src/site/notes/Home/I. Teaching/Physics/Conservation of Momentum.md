# Definition
The conservation of momentum is a special case when the net [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Impulse\|Impulse]] is zero. This implies that no [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Newton Laws\|external forces]] are acting on a body or a system. From the [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Impulse-Momentum Theorem\|impulse momentum theorem]], because the net impulse is zero, the [[Home/I. Teaching/Physics/Linear Momentum\|change in momentum]] is zero as well.

$$
\text{If $I=0$} \Rightarrow \Delta p =0
$$
